In honor of Joan Feynman: Women in astrophysics

Mon, January 12

Reader picks!

That our readers love

  • The Hunger Games Trilogy

    This trilogy is one I recommend if you're into post-apocalyptic books. They are three books total, that tell a story about a country that falls apart and forms districts. Each district is responsible for a different task, i.e. farming or mining. However, once a year the main district holds an event called 'The Hunger Games'; this event is to keep the other district in line. If I piqued your interest... don't be shy, pick up the first book!

    Suzanne Collins

  • The Game of Thrones

    This book is part of a series of five books and to me this is a fantasy medieval time of era. If you're into magic, mythological creatures, adventure, action and thrill mixed with a bit of suspension. You would love these books. They are very detailed and at times keeping you turning the pages. You won't be able to stop reading!

    George R. R. Martin

  • Harry Potter

    This book is the first one in a series of seven books. It tells a story of a young boy who discovers he is a wizard. He is invited to a school like no other, called Hogwarts. His name of course is, Harry Potter; and he's had a rough start... there are many more challenges ahead, but with the help of friends, Harry isn't alone anymore. A book filled with magic and adventure.

    J.K. Rowling

  • Twilight

    If you're into fantasy romance novels, Twilight would capture your heart. The first book of a series of four, it is a love story of girl and boy. Picture a small town, in a misty mountain area... girl moves there to live with her dad. She meets this young boy and he turns out to be a vampire. Yes, I said vampire; hints the fantasy part! They face pretty difficult challenges together. It'll keep you wanting to know what happens next!

    Stephenie Meyer

About the library

Libraries are essential establishments in practically every city, and the Triple Peaks Library is no exception. It first opened its doors way back in 2018, right as our humble town began bidding welcome to its very first citizens!

Since its initial opening, the library has seen nearly 342 monthly visitors. That's almost 50% of our population! So far, most citizens have enjoyed reading books and using our ample online resources.
